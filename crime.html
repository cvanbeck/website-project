AI's Use in Crime Prediction

AI predicts crime with 90 percent accuracy

Artificial Intelligence (AI) has become a powerful tool in predicting and preventing crime. AI-powered crime prediction systems use advanced algorithms to analyse large volumes of data, including crime rates and social media activity, to identify patterns and predict where crimes are likely to occur. With this technology, law enforcement agencies can prioritise resources and personnel to prevent crime and ensure public safety.

The use of AI in crime prediction has sparked debates around privacy concerns and bias in the algorithms used. However, the potential benefits of using AI to prevent crime cannot be ignored. Accuracy and results

A group of researchers from the University of Chicago have created a recently developed algorithm that can anticipate future crimes with an accuracy of around 90% for up to a week ahead. The artificial intelligence system has been trained to detect patterns related to violent and property crimes in Chicago, utilising public data from 2014 to 2016. It has shown the ability to accurately predict the degree of criminal activity several weeks in advance. The algorithm was also trained and tested on data from seven other major cities in the United States Los Angeles, San Francisco, Portland, Atlanta, Austin, Detroit and Philadelphia.

However, the results obtained from the AI also indicated the presence of racial prejudice in the responses of law enforcement officials throughout the city. "Such predictions enable us to study perturbations of crime patterns that suggest that the response to increased crime is biased by neighbourhood socio-economic status, draining policy resources from socio-economically disadvantaged areas, as demonstrated in eight major US cities" said Ishanu Chattopadhyay, assistant professor of medicine at the University of Chicago.
